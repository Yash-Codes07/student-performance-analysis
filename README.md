# Student Academic Performance Analysis

## Project Overview

This project analyzes factors that influence student academic performance using Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA). The objective is to identify relationships between GPA and various factors such as study time, attendance, parental support, tutoring, sports participation, music involvement, and volunteering activities.

The analysis was performed using Python and popular data analysis libraries to uncover patterns and insights from student performance data.

---

## Dataset

The dataset contains information about students, including:

* Age
* Gender
* Ethnicity
* Parental Education
* Study Time (Weekly)
* Absences
* Tutoring
* Parental Support
* Extracurricular Activities
* Sports Participation
* Music Participation
* Volunteering
* GPA
* Grade Class

## Analysis Questions

The project investigates the following questions:

1. Does more study time lead to a higher GPA?
2. Do absences affect GPA?
3. Does parental education affect student performance?
4. Does parental support affect GPA?
5. Do sports activities affect GPA?
6. Does participation in music affect GPA?
7. Does volunteering affect GPA?
8. Do students receiving tutoring perform better academically?

## Key Findings

* Students who study more hours per week generally achieve higher GPAs.
* Absences show a strong negative relationship with GPA.
* Higher parental support is associated with better academic performance.
* Students receiving tutoring tend to achieve slightly higher GPAs.
* Sports participation shows a small positive association with GPA.
* Music participation shows a small positive association with GPA.
* Volunteering shows a slight positive association with GPA.
* Parental education appears to have a relatively weak relationship with GPA compared to attendance and study habits.
